ISSUE 01 JULY 9, 2026

The Five-Minute Colleague

This week, Finance Forward introduces the first tool inside Vanguard's governed catalog — and proves, on one real task, that it earns its seat at the table in under five minutes.

At 4:47 on a Tuesday afternoon, an analyst on the FP&A team pasted eleven lines of raw variance data into a chat window and typed one sentence describing what the commentary needed to say. At 4:51, she was editing a draft, not writing one from a blank page. Four minutes. That is the entire pitch for this issue — not a vision of the future, but a Tuesday that ended four minutes shorter than it used to.

What's Actually Available, Starting Today

Your Vanguard-approved AI assistant is live and cleared for exactly this kind of work: drafting first-pass commentary, summarizing long documents, restructuring messy data into something presentable. It does not touch source systems, it does not see anything you don't paste into it, and it does not submit anything on your behalf. It drafts. You still sign.

If that sounds like a small thing, it is — deliberately. Every capability Vanguard adds this year will be this specific and this bounded, because trust is built one correctly-scoped tool at a time, not with a single sweeping rollout.

Try It Yourself

Open the assistant and paste this, adjusted to your own numbers:

"Here is this month's variance data for [cost center]: [paste rows]. Draft three sentences of commentary explaining the largest driver, in the tone we use for the monthly pack. Flag anything that looks like it needs a second look before I send it."

That's the whole prompt. Read the draft, correct what it gets wrong, send it in your own voice. The value isn't that it's right the first time — it's that editing is faster than authoring.

By the Numbers

30%

McKinsey's internal assistant, Lilli, sees weekly use from 75%+ of the firm's ~43,000 employees, averaging 17 uses per person per week, with reported time savings near 30% on the tasks it touches. Siloam is not McKinsey and Vanguard is not Lilli — the number worth taking isn't the 30%, it's that the habit, once formed, compounds weekly.

Source: McKinsey & Company reporting on Lilli internal usage, 2024–2025.
